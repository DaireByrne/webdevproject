Jamie Vardy Is Having A Party

After breaking Ruud van Nistelrooy's 12-year-old mark of scoring in 10 consecutive Barclays Premier League matches, Jamie Vardy is also on course to bag more records in the competition.

The goal against Manchester United was the Leicester City striker's 14th of the season after as many matches.

This rate of scoring by Vardy is ahead of the record-holders for the competition. The most goals scored by a player in a BPL season is 34, which was achieved by Alan Shearer, with Blackburn Rovers in 1994/95, and Andrew Cole, with Newcastle United the season before. After 14 matches of their record seasons Cole had scored 12 goals, while Shearer had nine.

"I've managed to get on the end of things, but it's my team-mates that have got me into the positions and all of our hard work that is making the difference."

Both of these records were set in a 42-match season. The records for the present status of a 38-match season is 31, which has been reached by three players: Shearer in 1995/96 with Blackburn, Cristiano Ronaldo with Man Utd in 2007/08 and Liverpool's Luis Suarez in 2013/14.

None of these three had scored as many Vardy had after 14 matches. Shearer and Suarez had struck one fewer, while Ronaldo had only eight goals at a similar stage.

In fairness to Suarez, he had reached his 13 goals from a mere nine matches, having been suspended for the first five matches of the 2013/14 season.

Vardy's tally of 14 goals in 14 matches is equal to that reached by Robin van Persie for Man Utd in 2011/12 and Sergio Aguero for Manchester City last season.

If Vardy continues his run of scoring in each match for the next six fixtures, he will match Suarez's record of reaching 20 goals by Matchweek 20.

He would be the first English striker to reach 20 goals so early, beating Kevin Phillips for Sunderland in 1999/00, who took 21 Matchweeks, and Shearer who took one matchweek more for Newcastle and Blackburn on three occasions.
